# LUXIONEX Verification Specification

## Version

v0.1

## Status

Draft — Foundational Verification Specification

---

# 1. Purpose

This specification defines the required structure, metadata, verification methods, and quality requirements for governance verification activities within LUXIONEX.

The objective is to ensure that governance requirements, controls, procedures, and evidence are independently verifiable, repeatable, measurable, and auditable.

This specification derives authority from:

Enterprise Constitution
        ↓
Governance Framework
        ↓
Verification Governance Standard
        ↓
Verification Specification

---

# 2. Scope

This specification applies to:

- Governance Verification
- Security Verification
- Architecture Verification
- Repository Verification
- Documentation Verification
- Compliance Verification
- Evidence Verification

---

# 3. Verification Objectives

Every verification activity SHALL:

- Validate governance implementation
- Confirm control effectiveness
- Verify compliance
- Produce objective evidence
- Support governance decisions
- Enable independent review

Verification SHALL remain evidence-based.

---

# 4. Required Metadata

Every verification record SHALL define:

- Verification Identifier
- Verification Title
- Version
- Status
- Verification Owner
- Approval Authority
- Verification Date
- Classification

---

# 5. Required Structure

Verification documents SHOULD include:

- Purpose
- Scope
- Verification Criteria
- Verification Method
- Verification Results
- Findings
- Evidence
- Recommendations
- Approval

---

# 6. Verification Requirements

Every verification SHALL define:

- Verification scope
- Success criteria
- Responsible authority
- Required evidence
- Verification method
- Expected outcome

Verification SHALL be reproducible whenever possible.

---

# 7. Governance Alignment

Every verification SHALL align with:

- Enterprise Constitution
- Governance Framework
- Applicable Standards
- Applicable Policies
- Related Controls
- Supporting Procedures

Verification SHALL support governance objectives.

---

# 8. Traceability

Verification SHALL maintain traceability to:

Requirement
        ↓
Policy
        ↓
Control
        ↓
Procedure
        ↓
Verification
        ↓
Evidence

Every relationship SHALL remain auditable.

---

# 9. Verification Methods

Verification MAY include:

- Technical Review
- Manual Inspection
- Automated Validation
- Security Assessment
- Compliance Assessment
- Internal Audit

Verification methods SHALL be documented.

---

# 10. Compliance

Compliance SHALL evaluate:

- Verification completeness
- Evidence quality
- Requirement coverage
- Control effectiveness
- Outstanding findings

Failed verification SHALL require corrective action.

---

# 11. Verification Lifecycle

Verification SHALL follow the lifecycle:

1. Planning
2. Preparation
3. Execution
4. Evidence Collection
5. Review
6. Approval
7. Reporting
8. Continuous Improvement

Historical verification records SHALL remain preserved.

---

# 12. Exceptions

Exceptions require:

- Documented justification
- Risk assessment
- Governance approval
- Defined review schedule

Approved exceptions SHALL remain traceable.
