## Version

v0.1

## Status

Draft — Foundational Policy Management Procedure

---

# 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the process for creating, reviewing, approving, publishing, maintaining, and retiring governance policies.

The objective is to ensure that policies remain authoritative, consistent, traceable, and aligned with organizational governance requirements.

---

# 2. Scope

This procedure applies to:

- Governance Policies
- Security Policies
- Architecture Policies
- Operational Policies
- Compliance Policies
- Repository Policies
- Documentation Policies

---

Policy Management Procedure

---

# 4. Roles and Responsibilities

## Policy Owner

Responsible for:

- Policy creation
- Policy maintenance
- Periodic review
- Policy accuracy

## Governance Authority

Responsible for:

- Policy approval
- Governance alignment
- Policy enforcement oversight

## Control Owner

Responsible for:

- Implementing related controls
- Providing verification evidence

---

# 5. Policy Lifecycle

Each policy SHALL follow:

Initiation
        ↓
Drafting
        ↓
Review
        ↓
Approval
        ↓
Publication
        ↓
Implementation
        ↓
Verification
        ↓
Maintenance
        ↓
Retirement

---

# 6. Policy Identification

Each policy SHALL contain:

- Policy ID
- Policy Name
- Purpose
- Owner
- Scope
- Classification
- Version
- Effective Date
- Review Date
- Status

---

# 7. Policy Development

Policy development SHALL consider:

- Governance Requirements
- Applicable Standards
- Risk Assessment
- Business Objectives
- Security Requirements
- Compliance Obligations

---

# 8. Policy Review Process

Policies SHALL be reviewed for:

- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Consistency
- Regulatory Alignment
- Technical Feasibility

---

# 9. Policy Approval

Approval SHALL verify:

- Authority Alignment
- Risk Considerations
- Implementation Requirements
- Control Applicability

Approved policies SHALL receive official status.

---

# 10. Policy Publication

Published policies SHALL:

- Have assigned ownership
- Be accessible to authorized users
- Include version information
- Maintain change history

---

# 11. Policy Traceability

Policies SHALL maintain relationships with:

Requirement
        ↓
Standard
        ↓
Specification
        ↓
Procedure
        ↓
Control
        ↓
Verification
        ↓
Evidence

---

# 12. Policy Enforcement

Policy enforcement SHALL be supported by:

- Defined Controls
- Operational Procedures
- Verification Activities
- Evidence Collection

---

# 13. Policy Change Management

Policy changes SHALL include:

- Change Description
- Reason for Change
- Impact Assessment
- Review Approval
- Updated Version

---

# 14. Policy Exceptions

Policy exceptions SHALL require:

- Exception Record
- Risk Assessment
- Approval Authority
- Expiration Date

---

# 15. Metrics

Policy management SHOULD measure:

- Policy Coverage
- Review Completion
- Compliance Status
- Exception Count
- Policy Effectiveness
